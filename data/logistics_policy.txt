Logistics Policy
1. Purpose

The purpose of this Logistics Policy is to establish standardized procedures for the management of transportation, warehousing, and distribution of goods. The policy ensures efficient operations, cost control, regulatory compliance, and timely delivery to customers and internal stakeholders.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third-party partners involved in logistics activities including procurement, warehouse operations, transportation, and inventory management.

3. Responsibilities
Logistics Department

The Logistics Department is responsible for planning, coordinating, and monitoring all logistics activities including shipping, receiving, and storage of goods.

Warehouse Staff

Warehouse personnel are responsible for:

Proper handling and storage of products

Accurate inventory tracking

Ensuring goods are packaged and prepared according to shipment requirements

Procurement Team

The Procurement Team coordinates with logistics to ensure suppliers meet delivery schedules and packaging standards.

4. Transportation Management

All shipments must be scheduled through the approved transportation management system (TMS). Preferred carriers should be used whenever possible to ensure cost efficiency and reliability.

Shipment documentation must include:

Bill of lading

Packing list

Shipping labels

Any regulatory documentation required for domestic or international transport.

5. Inventory Control

Inventory must be tracked using the company's warehouse management system (WMS). Regular cycle counts and periodic full inventory audits must be conducted to ensure accuracy.

Discrepancies must be reported to the Logistics Manager within 24 hours.

6. Packaging and Handling

All products must be packaged in accordance with internal packaging standards to prevent damage during transportation. Fragile or high-value items must be clearly labeled and handled according to special handling procedures.

7. Compliance and Safety

All logistics operations must comply with applicable local and international transportation regulations. Employees must follow workplace safety guidelines when handling goods, operating equipment, or loading and unloading shipments.

8. Performance Monitoring

Logistics performance will be measured through key metrics including:

On-time delivery rate

Transportation cost per shipment

Inventory accuracy

Order fulfillment cycle time

These metrics will be reviewed monthly to identify areas for improvement.

9. Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually by the Operations and Logistics Management teams to ensure alignment with business needs and regulatory requirements.
